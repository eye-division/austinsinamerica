Austins in Americas

Inspired by John Coleman's drive in 1959, five crew will drive three Austin 7's the 11,000 miles from Buenos Aires to New York City through twelve countries and two continents. Arrival in Times Square is planned for noon on May 31st 2013. Read the blogs below for progress and news.

30th January 2013

Posted on January 29, 2013 by jackp

Secure spares stowage

The unfortunate late removal of the engine and gearbox, allowed us to attend to a few issues. The improvement in performance, reduced noise level and peace of mind at having a chance to tweak and tighten everything were worth the time spent.

 

 

The back seat stowage swallowed all of the “Vince, big list of Spares” and provides a level platform for our luggage and equipment

 

Completed stowage compartment, where sum total of our luggage and camping gear will live

The trunk, that carries our camping gear, basic food stores and tools, safe from pilferage on the sea trip

9pm on the night before we left, replacing the faulty oil filter cartridge and flasher unit were the last two jobs completed

During front axle replacement

 

 

Drivers door, reshaped by a spanish windlass

 

The blown head gasket on New Years Eve, rather in the UK than in the Andes

 

 

Without back seats cushions the load space is adequate for our needs, the carpet will stop dents appearing in the body shell and help quieten the resonance

 

 

 

 

Bought on Ebay, we will discard the trunk in Buenos Aires.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Those final hours of fettling were nerve wracking. What else would go wrong, the final straw at 7pm was to find the oil filter cartridges for a change now and one or two en-route did not fit. Browsing rights at the local Motorist Centre and persuading them to stay open a while longer allowed a search that turned up two filters in time to fit and pack!

 

Replaced with a late Girling axle, heavy radius arms and Big 7 brake actuators with fabricated brackets, cables, I hope the axle will be an improvement and prove to be strong and trouble free

 

Sagging doors, were tweaked into position and the correct curvature by the simple expedient of galvanised wire secured at the extremities and twisted tight enough to banish droop and springing at the bottom.

 

 

 

Sounding like failing electrics, the rough running was eventually diagnosed as lack of compression on 2 and 3. On disassembly the cause was evident. Careful cleaning of the head and block did not reveal any cracks or lack of flatness, so the head and block were coated with Wellseal and replaced, since when, fingers have been crossed!

The engine installation, with air and oil filter, SU carb, now running like clockwork and giving 40mpg
