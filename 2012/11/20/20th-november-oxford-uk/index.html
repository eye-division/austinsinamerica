Austins in Americas

Inspired by John Coleman's drive in 1959, five crew will drive three Austin 7's the 11,000 miles from Buenos Aires to New York City through twelve countries and two continents. Arrival in Times Square is planned for noon on May 31st 2013. Read the blogs below for progress and news.

20th November Oxford, UK

Posted on November 20, 2012 by jackp

Despite the closeness of the departure date, things seem to be going backwards in some catagories.Â I will begin with “Dusty” and Stan.

Having noticed a slight sagging to the offside (car not Stan) he decided to lift the body off the chassis to investigate. It seems that due to an old repair to the offside chassis rail and the years of hammering over Route 66, the Gobi desert and hundreds of miles of “ripio” in Argentina the rail had bent upwards and allowed the rear of the car to settle in manner resembling the driver having been on an exclusive diet of doughnuts. Of course this is not really the case, is it?………

A lightening dash to David Williams our favourite supplier of secondhand parts, procured a Â straight chassis member and in short order the car was returned to its normal composure.

The rebuilt chassis with new offside rail

The body ready to be re-united with the chassis

The team have stayed in contact throughout the summer by Skype Conference Calling. This has been more or less successful, with some hilarious results, but has certainly made group decisions easier and kept the process more democratic. On Saturday the 17th of November, our most recent session, we ran through the plan and highlighted areas of concern and where more work is needed.

Changes were inevitable but recent information has meant a great deal of work has had to be done again, in particular regarding shipping. We had originally intended to put the cars onto a Ro-Ro ferry from Tilbury, however this is no longer possible because the shipping company are heavily committed to transporting new vehicles to South America by this method and no space is available for us. The alternative is a container, which although more secure, does mean we have had to find different shipping agents in the UK, who can collect, load the cars and deliver a container to the port.

Nearly there!

The destination port has also altered. We had intended to go to Montevideo, in order to avoid the horrors of Buenos Aires port, however ships now go to Zarate in Argentina, which may be a better port? Investigating port charges in Montevideo proved both difficult and seemed to require buying our cars back from the port authority, they were so high and very complex!

Together again and no saggy back end!

We have been given leads by both Phyllis and Mac Macnie and Santiago Sanchez Ortega to agents in Argentina and are exploring how we might achieve the safe and not too expensive arrival of the cars.

Meanwhile, while I was supposed to be attending to my car, I have been waylaid by the shipping crisis and the wedding of a second daughter this year on December 1st. I have in the last two days removed the front axle, steering box, sunshine roof and replaced the oil pressure gauge. I hope to be able to put the axle back in the next two weeks and perhaps finish the other jobs by mid December. The front seats are away for re-upholstering and the steering wheel for recovering. I also have a newly built dynamo to fit with an attendant 12 volt solid state regulator. This will entail new bulbs all round and some changes to coil, horn, wiper motor and petrol gauge.
